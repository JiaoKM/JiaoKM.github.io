Research
Multi-stream domain adversarial prototype network for integrated smart roller TBM main bearing fault diagnosis across various low rotating speeds
Xingchen Fu, Keming Jiao, Jianfeng Tao, Chengliang Liu. Reliability Engineering & System Safety p. 110284. Elsevier, 2024
Domain adaptation in fault diagnosis of roller bearing
October,  2022  - present
With the great development of AI technology, the application of deep learning has brought too many realms to a new era. Fault diagnosis is one of them. But there still exists some challenges, such as domain-shift, which prevents one effective method to apply on different working situations. There are two mainstream methods. One is difference measurement and another is adversarial training. We choose the latter one as the methodology to solve the domain-shift. We proposed a novel dual-stream multi-channel neural network based on adversarial learning, and evaluated the effectiveness of it on the fault datasets of roller state under different rotational speed conditions. Our work continues.

In the winter school, every group should completed a CV and a NLP project. I mainly participated in the NLP project. In the natural language processing project, I extracted titles, abstracts and articles from the COVID-19 Open Research Dataset (CORD-19) and mined data and knowledge from these words. There were 4 steps to complete this project: Scan all JSON files in the given dataset and extract texts from relevant parts; Use different tokenizers to traverse the extracted texts and segment the texts into words; Use different models to build word representations for each word; Display the knowledge and data mined from the word representations through various methods.
